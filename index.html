Getting Back on Track with the Lord

by Joanni B.

Friday, 26 June 2026

Ever felt like you just cannot feel the presence of God anymore, or just simply lost the desire to seek Him?

Well, this page is just for you! My name is Joanni, and on this page I will be sharing my journey, along with some advice, on how I got back on track with the Lord - that is with the help of the Holy Spirit of course.

Let's get right into it!

My Story

I used to believe that the longer one walks with the Lord, the less one would experience spiritual lows; boy was I deceived.

I have been walking with the Lord for about seven years now, and I never would have imagined I would experience such a difficult season in my life which almost cost me my relationship with the Lord. It is only, by the grace of God, that I made it out of that season still holding onto my faith. These are some of the essential actions the Holy Spirit led me to do during this period of my life in order to restore my flame for Jesus:

  1. Being Honest in Prayer
  2. Meditating on Biblical Truths
  3. Seeking Counselling

1. Being Honest in Prayer

Quite often, as believers, we tend to forget God's fatherly attribute. We structure our prayers to include words of exaltation, thanksgiving, praises, etc - which are not wrong at all. However, when you're going through a 'spiritual-low', these words might not flow as easily.

It is crucial to be honest with the Lord in these moments. No fancy words. No pretence. Simply honesty and transparency. The Psalms are a great place to start if you need some guidance, which I will reference in the next topic.

When you struggle to find the words to speak, praying in silence or simply crying in His presence, like Hanah, (1 Samuel 1:12-13) still moves the heart of God. Even more so, the Holy Spirit interceeds for us when we fall short in prayer.

Romans 8:26 says:

"Likewise the Spirit also helpeth our infirmities: for
we know not what we should pray for as we ought:
but the Spirit itself maketh intercession for us with
groanings which cannot be uttered."

Therefore, be honest when you pray to your Father in heaven.

2. Meditating on Biblical Truths

The Word of God carries healing, restoration, conviction and deliverance. In seasons when you feel low and disconnected, the enemy seeks to attack two important areas of our faith: our prayer-life and time we set aside to meditate on the Word. This is because he knows that these two areas are our weapons against him and it is where we are revived.

During my season of feeling rock-bottom in my faith, I struggled to open my bible, but it was the Holy Spirit who continuously worked up in me a hunger and thirst to find myself in the scriptures again. The first place I went to was to the Psalms of David. Throughout the book of Psalms, the writers express deep and honest emotions like anger, rage, sorrow, feeling lonely and confused, which you might resonate with. One Psalm that specifically stood out for me was Psalm 51 . Furthermore, meditating on the Word will give you words to pray unto the Lord when you are lost of words.

Reading the Word also allows you to speak God's truths over your life. In moments when you are weak, the enemy will try to plant lies in you mind, thus, meditating on the truths on God will nulify the influence of the enemies lies and remind you of who you are in Christ.

3. Seeking Counselling

Lastly, the Holy Spirit led me to seek godly counseling during my season of weakness. Speaking to someone trustworthy, who is available to lend a listening ear married with biblical based advice and prayer, fosters the ability to release emotions, share what you are experiencing and receive clarity or just a shoulder to cry on. The Lord never intended for man to walk alone in their Christian walk, but instead to be in community and fellowship with other believers. Therefore, seeking godly counseling and being surrounded by a faith community, cancels any opening for depression or severe disconnection to take place.

In conclusion, the topics discussed here are not in any particular order of least or more importance, and neither are they the only ways the Holy Spirit helps one overcome spiritual lows, but are what he ministered to me during my season of weakness. I hope this page as blessed you and I want to remind you the Lord is your redeemer and the lifter of your head.

Isaiah 44:22:

"I have swept away your offenses like a cloud,
your sins like the morning mist. Return to me,
for I have redeemed you.”
